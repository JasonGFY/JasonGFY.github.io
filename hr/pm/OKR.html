Performance Management

OKR


OKR as the popular performance management tool, has been mentioned in so many occasions and scenarios.

OKR consists of two parts: Objectives and Key results. First, let's talk about the objuctives. what characteristics should a good objective have? It should be specific and clear, have a certain degree of difficulty, and it should enable employees to understand to do what, why do it, and what significance do these actions hold for the employees themself. and we also can use SMART principle to measure the quality of the OKR.
When you implement the OKR system. Do not set too many objectives, limit it to no more than 5; Rank the objectives by the importance, it will make the team more focused; During the evaluation period, do not change the objectives, but key results can be changed. we should distinguish between tasks and objectives, completing tasks doesn't necessarily bring value, but achieving objectives will definitely bring value.
Tracking the progress of OKR There are usually 3 methods. one is the kanban method, team can use physical kanban, or intranets to store progress information, which can be accessed publicly, to remind team. one is to conduct regular review through PDCA, and another one is to track the confidence index of OKR. on matter which method is adopted, the main purpose of review is to make the team focus on how to complete the remaining unfinished ojectives.
OKR differs from traditional KPIs in the way of formulating, OKR involves two parts: top-down and bottom-up. Objectives are set by senior management, then cascade down to each level and each team. when the objectives breakdown to the level of employees, encourage them to discuss objectives and set one at least. Team can hold a meeting to set objecives, everyone need to attend and actively provide suggestions, each suggestion can be write on sticky note, and post together on the board, remove the duplicates, and vote to select 3 to 5 final objectives.
please remember, OKR is not equivalent to the performance evaluation, it's a objective management system, which can ensure close teamwork among the team, promote the development of the organization, and measure contributions.
