Competency Model


Competency Definition

A Competency is an underlying characteristics of an individual that is causally related to criterion-referenced effective and / or superior performance in a job or situation. Underlying characteristics Five types of Competency characteristics: 1. Montives. 2. Traits 3. Self-concept 4. Knowledge 5. Skill Casual relationships

  • Reaction

Learning Organization

Learning Organization theory was proposed by Peter Senge, in his works The Fifth Discipline - The Art and practice of the Learning Organization. Building a learning organization means shifting the organization from a "passive adapation" to "active learning". This transformation requires 5 key practices:

  • Personal Mastery
  • Improving Mental Models
  • Building Shared Vision
  • Team Learning
  • System Thinking


Competency Model

The core of the competency model theory is to use the competency characteristics that are directly related to job performance to replace traditional intelligence tests or educational backgrounds, in order to predict employees' job performance and provide scientific basis for talent recruitment, training development, performance assessment, promotion selection and other work. The competency model is a set of competency characteristics that can achieve high performance, extracted for a specific position or organizational level. It usually consists of three elements:
Competency dimension: The name of the competency category;
Behavioral indicators: The specific behavioral manifestations corresponding to the dimension;
Grading standards: The hierarchical division of the behavioral indicators.
